FAC 451
JANE STANTON: Love Will Tear Us Apart - A History Of The Hacienda
May 99

Additional Notes:

"I was really pleased to see Fac 451 listed on your web site. 'Love Will Tear Us Apart' - A History of the Hacienda was given the official Fac 451 number by Tony Wilson, as it was "for" 51 (the Hacienda). This was the first documentary that I produced and directed. It was a half-hour documentary which was transmitted by Granada Television May 1999. All the interviews were new interviews, and for many, it was the first time that they had spoken about their experiences of the Hacienda. The documentary is dedicated to Rob Gretton who sadly died a few days before it was aired. With the Hacienda's demolition is iminent, there is talk of this documentary being shown again nationally." - Jane Stanton

Notes from a viewer (David Ellcock): "It featured interviews with loads of people involved with the club, including Bernard Sumner, Peter Hook, Tony Wilson, Graeme Park, Mike Pickering etc. so it must have been made with their blessing. At the end of the programme, just before the credits, they showed the "FAC 51: The Hacienda" logo. In the bottom right hand corner of the screen, at the same time as the logo was showing, it said "FAC 451".

True to form, it seems that FAC 451 has also been allocated to the 'Party People film's reconstructed Hacienda set and filming party/session, as mentioned on FAC 433 and as shown on the ticket and t-shirts for the session.

"Tony Wilson marked the occasion by commissioning Factory designer Trevor Johnson to create special FAC451 (the FAC number assigned to the Haçienda set) tickets and t-shirts. However the man himself was nowhere to be seen on the evening. 'It may surprise some people, but I'm actually unbearably and painfully self-conscious' he said, the next day. Hmm..." (from FAC 433).

"One minor adjustment to your site. John Walsh designed (the) FAC 451 (tickets) not Trevor Johnson, although Trevor did get me the job..." (John Walsh).
